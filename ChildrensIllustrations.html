Children's
Illustrations

When approaching children’s illustration I always think back to the types of picturebooks I loved
as a kid. I adored anything that seemed to have endless detail, pages you could keep coming
back to and finding something new in each time. I always loved bright, bold, quirky designs. My
favourite illustrator has always been Lauren Child with her collaged style, full of patterns and
whimsical details even in the most normal settings. I find that children’s illustration has the most
scope for using my personal style because the more colourful and out there it is, the better.
Also, due to the nature of telling a story, children’s books are the best place for me to express
my interest in fashion and interior design through the characters and settings I create.

All the illustrations you see here are from a personal project.
During university I started creating work for an original story
about a postwoman who’s scared of dogs and overcomes
her fear through meeting many dog-owning families.
These families I created all have a key shared
interest or theme that I really hyperbolise to show
a strong sense of belonging. I think I thrive
with a theme as it gives me the opportunity
to really experiment outwards from one central idea
and do some worldbuilding.

In the future, I want to create more scenes showing the emotion of the story
and the main protagonist’s development. For now I have been enjoying
honing my style through the family scenes.
