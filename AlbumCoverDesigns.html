Album Cover
Designs

When approaching album cover design I try to think of an overall theme or genre to express with a key motif because you want an album cover to immediately translate what the listener should expect from the album. It also serves as a small artwork in the home especially if printed onto a record sleeve. So I try to treat it as such by using pleasing layouts. Since I’m working with a square I enjoy playing around with symmetry and balance.

The project you see here is a personal one for a fictional girl band I created called ‘The Rosehips’.
This trio is a girl punk/RiotGrrrl band so I enjoyed riffing off of stereotypical notions of femininity and turning them
on their heads. To get into the headspace I listened to a lot of RiotGrrrl music from the
1990s and read the manifestos of the movement.

The first album cover is a play on the idea of a teaparty and hosting as an expectation for women.
I created detailed tonal drawings of retro desserts with coloured pencil to remind us of an era when
women had much stricter expectations of being the perfect housewife and hostess. I then turned it into
a twisted tea party by combining bold black marker drawings of insects into the mix.

The second album cover is a play on the girlhood motif of a doll’s house. Usually used to practice for a future
life of being the perfect housewife, here I designed a chaotic dollhouse-one fit for a punk band to live in.
With vivid colours, mind boggling patterns and a messy party kitchen- the imaginary owner of this house
knows how to have fun outside of society’s expectations. I used collage and multimedia techniques here to
create a playful design.

The last album cover is a play on decorative ceramics. Quite a sensible feminine collection to have in your home, often found in antique stores with pieces from a time gone by before the women’s lib movement. I turned this on its head by creating a hot pink tiger painted design for the plate to represent women’s strength and rebellion. It’s also a play on words since it’s for the RiotGrrrl movement and of course tigers make a grrr sound.
