Stationery
Designs

When approaching stationery design I think about when I use cute stationery the most, which is when I’m writing letters. Letterwriting has long been a hobby of mine and I love to make my happy mail package the most cheerful as possible. It always helps to have the right stationery set. My go to shop used to be Paperchase which I’m sad to have seen go. But I still think of the ideal writing set model they had which was often 20x sheets of writing paper, 10x envelopes and 10x sealing stickers. Meaning you could write 10 two page long letters and seal them all. I also loved how they released collections with key themes across the board like under sea or woodland which would be printed in slightly varying ways on notebooks, pencil cases, stickers, etc. This cheerful way of approaching surface pattern design is something that I carry through to my designs.

One of the projects you see on this page is a letter writing set called ‘Rainbow Rainforest’. It is inspired
by a university project called ‘Folklore’ where I chose to focus on aboriginal Australian folklore such as the tales
of the rainbow serpent and how the birds got their colours. I started this project by creating a myriad of patterns
inspired by markmaking in aboriginal paintings and instruments. I then edited these patterns to be every colour
of the rainbow, printed them out, and started work on collaging animals out of these handmade supplies.
It was a painstaking process of cutting out hundreds of snake scales and bird feathers but I think it was worth it.
I then decided to create a letter writing set out of these. I chose to do two differing writing paper designs so that the
user could mix and match papers within the same letter. I paired this with ten simple dark green envelopes so that
the multicoloured sealing sticker I designed would pop off of it.

The other project you see is a couple of vinyl stickers I have designed and had printed.
I am working on creating various sticker designs for my Etsy shop, a place where I get to share
my stationery designs. I think this is a great format for my work as I create colourful,
eyecatching designs that I think would bring a little bit of novelty and play to everyday
situations like school or work. One design is called ‘Space Payphone’ and the other is called
‘Pear Library’. I pulled these designs from my personal sketchbook practice where I let my mind
wander. I tend to create simpler, doodle-like illustrations in my personal sketchbook which can
work well for this context.
